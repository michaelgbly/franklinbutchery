# Owner Interview — Key Insights

**Source:** Pre-opening interview with owners (Greg & Lori) and Carlos
**Raw transcript:** `memory/context/owner-interview-transcript.md`

---

## Origin Story

The concept was born from a friendship between two families. Greg & Lori's daughter and Carlos & Aubrey's daughter met in kindergarten/first grade at a school carnival. The parents became close friends. Carlos was already a butcher. Greg and Lori kept running into Carlos and Aubrey at Whole Foods trying (and failing) to get the cuts they wanted. The lightbulb moment: "What are we doing? We need a butcher shop in this town." They found a large-footprint location in Franklin, and the scale of the concept grew to fill it — private dining room, store-within-a-store merch, the full experience.

**Key quote (Greg):** "We wanted this to be unique... not just a place where they slung meat. It was a place you'd come in, it was warm, you'd want to spend time there, you'd want to come back, you'd want to chat, you'd want to hang out."

---

## First Impression Goal

Owners want guests to walk in and think: **"Damn, this is cool."**
Keywords from interview: unexpected, unique, elevated experience, worth the incremental spend.

---

## The Five Pillars (Confirmed Order of Importance)

1. **Butcher case** — local meat
2. **Seafood** — fresh from Maine, not frozen (except possibly shrimp)
3. **Sandwiches** — names are based on the owners' children (family story)
4. **Beer & wine**
5. **Meals To Go / prepared foods** — owners said "I think that'll be number one" and "that is a pillar"

---

## Meals To Go — Critical Nuances

- **Refrigerated, chef-driven meals** designed for reheating at home
- Meat-centric focus (not mini meatloafs — real meat-forward dinners)
- Examples mentioned: lasagna, spaghetti and meatballs, meatloaf, mac and cheese, bolognese sauce, orzo salads, pasta sides, potatoes
- Owner prefers the model where "we cook it for you and then you take it home"
- Also retail add-ons: cheeses, mother sauces, elevated meal components
- **NOT a restaurant** — sandwich + side might take ~8 minutes, but don't advertise cooked-to-order steak dinners
- Cooked-to-order capability is a "secret" — people learn about it organically, not advertised

## Sourcing Details (From Owner's Mouth)

### Beef & Pork — Bear Creek Farms
- ~2 full cows per week from Bear Creek, plus ~half from Simpsons ("two and a half")
- Also getting half a cow that's grass-fed (separate from the grain-finished Bear Creek cattle)
- Fully traceable — "down to the cow," identified by number (e.g., "272")
- Pasture-raised, grass-fed with grain finishing at the end
- Vet on staff blends the finishing feed — "not corn, not green, nutritionally blended"
- Animals drink only well water from the property
- Pork also from Bear Creek

### Poultry — Springer Mountain
- Local Tennessee/Georgia area
- Premium poultry brand, described as "like a co-op"
- Owners not planning to highlight poultry sourcing on website as much as beef and seafood

### Seafood
- All from Maine
- Fresh, not frozen (shrimp may be exception)

### In-House
- All cures blended in-house
- All spices are organic
- Bacon and cured items made in-house

## Dry-Aging / Cut Room

- Fully glass-walled, fully visible to guests
- Everything cut and hung/aged in the same room
- Himalayan salt wall — helps with air quality, humidity capture, ionization
- **Language note from owner:** Talk about "ionization of the air for the dry age process" — don't talk about bacteria. Keep it approachable.
- Guests can point to specific pieces, request custom cut thickness

## Merch / Store-Within-a-Store

- **Roper Field** — handmade leather goods, made in Franklin
- **Lavender Row** — lavender products, sold under the Roper Field umbrella
- Franklin Butchery branded merch
- Curated dry goods
- Owners were still deciding whether to feature on the butchery website or keep separate

---

## Private Dining & Events

- **Not a reservation system** — event-based, inquiry-driven
- Guests reach out via email/contact form with what they're envisioning
- Options: rent the room only, or full catering (sandwiches to full dinners)
- Contact form should go to the info/family email that someone is actively checking
- **Supper club events:** slow launch, announced via Instagram/email, aspirational goal of regular Friday/Saturday dinners eventually
- Comparable reference: Perrin's (local restaurant doing Thu/Fri/Sat dinners)

---

## Grand Opening

- No sales or discounts — community-forward, not discount-led
- Possibly first 50–100 guests get hats or similar giveaway
- Live music, something for kids
- Soft openings the week leading up
- Instagram ramp-up critical in the weeks before

---

## Demographic Insight

- **"More women than you think actually go in and are buying the meats and the seafoods."** — Lori
- The elevated experience was intentionally designed to appeal to a broader audience, not just the stereotypical male butcher-shop customer

---

## Education Vision

- YouTube and Instagram short-form videos: Carlos cutting meat, Greg (chef) showing what to do with it
- Newsletters and blogs: "Have you ever tried this with this cut?"
- Guests invited to chat with Carlos and the team — especially at the butcher counter
- Goal: open people up beyond their default purchases (most people default to ribeye)
